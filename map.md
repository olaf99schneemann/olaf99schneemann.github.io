# Detailed Insights & Methods from Granola Video

## Root Insight
- Lack of focus isn't the problem
- Problem: Inability to direct existing attention
- Brain's management system = **Executive Functions (EF)**

## Executive Functions (EF)
### Role of EF
- Direct attention
- Control impulses
- Maintain goals and plans
### Symptoms of weak EF
- Acting on every impulse
- Inability to prioritize
- Task switching and distractions

## Causes of EF Weakness
### Environmental Factors
- Information overload
- Constant notifications & digital distractions
- Doomscrolling and multitasking
### Developmental (ADHD)
- Inborn executive dysfunction
- Dopamine imbalance
- Remains even without external triggers

## Core Problems & Solutions
### 1. Anxiety/Impulse Loop
- **Cause**
  - Overloaded working memory
  - High cognitive load
- **Effects**
  - Restlessness, constant task switching
  - Incomplete tasks, mental clutter
- **Solutions**
  - **Kaizen Log**
    - Morning brain dump
    - Pick a "daily highlight"
    - Helps prioritize consciously
  - **Impulse Log**
    - Note every impulse without acting on it
    - Use 2-3 words
    - Builds pause between thought & action
  - **External Systems**
    - Notes app at desk
    - Message self with hashtags on the go

### 2. Lack of Clarity & Energy Management
- **Problem with meetings**
  - Draining, hard to focus, forget important info
- **Tool: Granola**
  - Real-time transcription
  - Detects tasks, deadlines, key decisions
  - Works across Zoom, Meet, Teams, In-person
  - Quietly integrates into workflow
- **Workflow Fix**
  - Categorize tasks into 4 types: `Urgent`, `Deadline-based`, `Admin`, `Creative`
  - **Use sprints** instead of rigid time slots
    - Tackle urgent & deadlines early in day
    - Admin during low energy times
    - Creative work at peak creative hours

### 3. Low Boredom Tolerance
- **Why it happens**
  - Dopamine receptors find much stimulation = long-term imbalance
  - Modern lifestyle breaks pain-pleasure balance
- **Effects**
  - Everything feels dull
  - Reactivity to distraction increases
- **Fix: 10-Minute Focus Game**
  - Set visual timer for 10 mins
  - Task open in front, no movement allowed
  - $+10$ if you stay, $-10$ if you move
  - Gradually increase time each week

## Mental Overload Strategy
- Don't rely on brain to remember
- Use it to think, analyze, and solve
- Offload tasks and info to external systems

## Dopamine Hygiene
- **Avoid overstimulation**
  - Limit mindless scrolling, passive consumption
  - Allow boredom to recalibrate dopamine levels
- Actively train brain to handle low-reward tasks

## Final Advice
- Don't fix everything at once
- Start with one method
- Build slow, mark progress
- Re-visit when needed and adjust
